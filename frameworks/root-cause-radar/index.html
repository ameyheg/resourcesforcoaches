The Root-Cause Radar

An evolution of 'The 5 Whys' designed specifically for unpacking deeply held limiting beliefs without traumatizing the client.

The Root-Cause Radar

“The 5 Whys” is a popular corporate framework for finding the root cause of a manufacturing error. But human beings are not machines. Relentlessly asking a client “Why?” can feel interrogative and aggressive.

The Root-Cause Radar is an evolution of this concept, designed to unpack deeply held limiting beliefs gently, while preventing the client from feeling defensive or dysregulated.

1. The Surface Emotion

What is the acute feeling they are bringing into the session today? (e.g., “I feel intense anxiety about launching my new coaching program next week.”)

2. The First Why (The Trigger)

Identify the immediate stressor. “What is it about launching that specifically causes the anxiety?” (Response: “I’m worried people in my network will judge me.”)

3. The Second Why (The Meaning)

Identify the internal narrative. “If they were to judge you… what does that mean?” (Response: “It means they think I’m a fraud, which means I’ll fail.”)

4. The Radar Flip (The Crucial Step)

This is where the framework diverges. Instead of pushing deeper into the pain, you pivot to honoring the belief. The belief exists to protect the client. “When in your life did worrying about ‘being judged’ actually keep you safe?”

Why This Works:

Honoring the belief’s original, protective purpose disarms the ego. Once the client realizes the anxiety is just an outdated safety mechanism acting up, they can thank it for its service and choose a new, empowering belief without fighting themselves.
