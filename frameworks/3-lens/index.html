The 3-Lens Perspective Shift

A conflict resolution tool based on narrative therapy, used when a client is heavily entrenched in being 'right'.

What it is and why it works

Often, clients come to sessions stuck in a conflict with a partner, a boss, or a co-founder. They are firmly entrenched in the “Microsight” view—they are completely convinced their version of the story is the objective truth.

This framework works by forcing the brain to step out of its own narrative ego. It creates deep empathy and helps the client see the structural dynamics of a conflict rather than just the personal slights.

Step-by-Step Execution

1. Lens 1: The Microscope (Self)

Allow the client to fully air their side of the street. Ask: “What is the story you are telling yourself about what they did? How did it make you feel?“

2. Lens 2: The Telescope (The Other)

Force the perspective shift. Ask: “I want you to become their lead defense attorney for a moment. You have to defend their actions in court. What is the most generous, logical argument for why they behaved the way they did?“

3. Lens 3: The Drone (The System)

Zoom out completely from the individuals. Ask: “Imagine you are a drone looking down at this relationship from 5,000 feet. You don’t care about either person’s feelings. What are the unspoken ‘rules’ of this relationship that are broken? What is the structural flaw here?”

Examples in Practice

Executive Coaching Example: A founder is arguing with a CTO about shipping a feature. Microscope: “The CTO is being lazy and slowing us down.” Telescope: “The CTO is terrified of shipping broken code because the last time we did, the board yelled at him.” Drone: “We have no clear system for balancing speed with QA. This isn’t a personality conflict; it’s a missing operational protocol.”

💡 Try This Today

Have the client physically change locations. Have them sit in their normal chair for Lens 1, move to a different chair for Lens 2, and stand up against the wall for Lens 3. Physical movement anchors the psychological shift.

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rescuing the client: The client will struggle hard with Lens 2 (The Telescope). They will want to keep blaming. Hold the silence. Make them find the generous interpretation.
