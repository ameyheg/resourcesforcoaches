The Difference Between Coaching, Consulting, and Therapy

Blurring these lines is the fastest way to ruin a client relationship, burn yourself out, or face ethical/legal issues. Let’s make this crystal clear.

Therapy (The Past):

Therapy is medical and psychological. It heals trauma. It asks, “How did the past create the present?” It explores the root causes of depression, anxiety, and deep-seated wounds. If a client is not highly functional, they need a therapist, not a coach.

Consulting (The Answer):

Consulting is advice-driven. It asks, “How do I fix this specific problem?” The consultant is the expert. They look at a business, say “Your sales process is broken,” and then write a playbook to fix it. Consultants give the answers.

Coaching (The Future):

Coaching is forward-looking and generative. It asks, “Where are we now, and how do we get to where we want to be?” The coach is NOT the expert in the client’s life; the client is. The coach is an expert in the process of discovery. Coaches ask the questions.

The Golden Rule:

If you find yourself constantly telling the client exactly what to do step-by-step, you are consulting. If you are diagnosing their childhood trauma, you are practicing unlicensed therapy. Stop. Return to the coaching stance: ask a powerful question, and wait.
